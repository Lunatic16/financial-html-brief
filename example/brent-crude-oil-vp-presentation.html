Verdict

Brent Crude is stabilizing at $103–106/bbl as markets accept a prolonged Hormuz closure as the base case. The intraday spike to $106.50 and retreat to $103.73 suggests equilibrium is being found within this band, not a breakdown of the supply shock thesis.

Infrastructure escalation is real and accelerating in strategic targeting. The Dubai airport attack represents a qualitative shift — Iranian drones are now demonstrating capability and willingness to strike civilian energy infrastructure that directly affects global commerce.

Coalition-building is the key variable. Japan's refusal to deploy naval assets is a meaningful datapoint. If NATO allies follow suit, the U.S. bears the diplomatic and military burden alone, extending the conflict timeline and strengthening the $120+ upside scenario.

The 4–6 week timeline from the U.S. Energy Secretary aligns precisely with Goldman Sachs' trigger for $100+ pricing. We are currently in that window. The next 3 weeks determine whether this resolves into the $70–80 bear case or extends into the $120–130 upside scenario.
